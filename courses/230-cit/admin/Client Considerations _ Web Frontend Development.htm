Consider Your Clients

When planning any web project - a site, an application or other content to be delivered via the Internet - there are fundamental consideration and questions that need to be answered concerning those who visit your site. These people are know as the target audience, who are referred to individually as the client.

When planning your own site and thinking about the audience as a whole or a client individually, consider each of the following:

Who are you trying to reach?

  • Age range
  • Occupation(s) (student, professional, etc...)
  • Income
  • Family status
  • Experience level with the topic of your site and its content

Where is the client located and what is the client doing when he/she visits your site? For example:

  • Sitting at home using a desktop or laptop machine.
  • Laying bed, using a tablet or phone, prior to sleep.
  • Commuting to work on public transportation using a tablet or phone.
  • In a restaurant, using their phone, while eating.
  • In their public library using their laptop.

What does the client want?

What is she trying to do? What content is he trying to find? Both of these can be described using a scenario (description of the client's actions). Consider the following client wants and how a web site could best meet them:

  • What does it cost to ride the bus from Golden Gate park to Fisherman's wharf?
  • Where is the nearest ATM machine?
  • What are the hours for my neighborhood pharmacy?
  • Does that store carry a polo shirt in my size? What color options exist? Prices?
  • How do I make a CSS-driven dropdown navigation bar?
  • What's the weather going to be this weekend at the trail I'm planning to hike?

Where is the client located?

Does the visitor's geographic location impact the content delivery? Consider how a web site could be affected by clients in these situations:

  • I'm driving and need directions to the hospital.
  • How far from the airport is the hotel?
  • I need someone to wash my windows.
  • Is there a veterinary clinic nearby?
  • I'd like that pizza delivered.

When is the site being visited?

How does the time affect the content delivery?

  • It's 9pm and I just broke my leg.
  • It's 1am, the pizza delivery is at the door and I don't have any cash.
  • It's 2pm and I need a haircut before my job interview at 4:30.
  • It's 6:45pm and I want tickets to the 7:20 movie.

How are clients accessing the site?

What are the capabilities and limitations of their device?

  • A desktop machine, fast broadband connection.
  • A Kindle Fire HD using wi-fi at McDonalds
  • An iPhone 8 Plus using 4G LTE
  • A Samsung Note 8 with a commuter train wi-fi connection
  • A Windows Surface Pro 4 using a cell phone hot spot
